How To Build A Mobile.

ID Number:



    Every mobile type needs a unique ID number. You need your Zone Number to do this properly. If you are zone 25, then the first mobile in your zone will be mobile 2500 (n*100). Then every mobile you add from there should increment the number. Mobile 2501, 2502, and so on.
Name:    Every mobile needs a name. Such as, "The Cityguard".
Gender:    Self explanatory. The only options are "male" and "female".
Targets:    What you type to identify the mobile. Like, "city", "guard", "cityguard".
Title:    What you see in the room. "The Cityguard is standing here."
Description:

    What you see when you look at the mobile. This can be a short description, or it can be an entire paragraph, depending on how descriptive you want it.
Level:
    Every mobile needs a level from 1-99. This is an indicator of how tough it is.
Speed:



    How fast your mobile walks. Rated on a scale from 0-60000. Zero being motionless, sixty-thousand meaning the mobile would move at every possible opportunity (once per second). An average rating is 1000, which indicates movement about once per minute.
Alignment:

    Indicates if your mobile is light or dark (good or evil). Rated on a scale from 0-100. Zero would be completely dark, fifty neutral, and one-hundred perfectly light.
Experience:    Level-up points earned from killing the mobile.
Gold:    Amount of money earned from killing the mobile.

Mobile Statistics.

The following stats you should attribute to your mobile based on its level. This can be done by following the stat balance charts shown below here. Every mobile has a fixed amount of health and mana, and five innate stats that are not modified by equipment. They also have four stats that are modified by equipment. For characters, these four "equipment stats" are ONLY modified by equipment, and are zero if no equipment is worn. But mobiles can have equipment stats without actually using equipment. This is necessary because every mobile needs to have sufficient equipment stats to be strong enough for its level, even if it is unarmed. So keep in mind that the four equipment stats have two modifiers for mobiles: The innate value, and the equipment value.

Health:      Amount of hit points for receiving damage.
Mana:      Amount of magic points for casting spells.
        
Innate Stats:




     
Strength      (Attack Power)
Dexterity    (Dodging & Defense)
Constitution    (Health Regeneration)
Intelligence    (Spell Power)
Wisdom    (Mana Regeneration)
        
Equipment Stats:



     
Hitroll        (Weapon Aim)
Damroll    (Weapon Damage)
Armorclass    (Absorb Damage)
Resistance    (Absorb Magic)

Formulas To Calculate Average Stats.

You can use the following formulas to calculate the complete average for every statistic a mobile might have at a given level. You can of course make mobiles much weaker or stronger than average. However using these numbers should give you a point of reference.

Health      (Level * 100)   + 100
Mana      (Level * 100)   + 100
            
Strength      (Level * 2)   + 2
Dexterity      (Level * 1.5)   + 2
Constitution      (Level * 1.5)   + 2
Intelligence      (Level * 1)   + 1
Wisdom      (Level * 1)   + 1
            
Hitroll      (Level / 5)   + 1
Damroll      (Level / 4)   + 1
Armorclass      (Level * 10)   + 10
Resistance      (Level / 3)    

Experience      (Level * 1000)    
Gold      (Level * 10)   / 2

Remember, mobiles can have equipment stats without wearing equipment. Their total Hit/Dam/AC/MR will be the sum of their innate value plus whatever is added by their equipment. Keep this in mind when calculating stats for mobiles as well as items.

Mobile Equipment.

Mobiles can have items that they have in their inventory, or items that they automatially equip. All you need to do is specify which item is to be loaded, and how often it loads. This is done on a scale from 0-10000. A zero would indicate that they never load with the item, and a ten-thousand would mean that they always do. For example:

ITEM 3100 10000
WEAR 1000 5000

 Item 3100 is a Red Rose, and item 1000 is a Backpack. With these numbers, the mobile would always load with a Red Rose in their inventory, and half the time, they would load and equip a backpack.

Mobile Flags.

Flags can say many things about your mobile, and what they can or cannot do. You can make up your own flags even if they don't exist in the code, and they will be added by the coders. Here are some examples of flags that exist already:

MOBILE      Your mobile will move around randomly.
AGGRO      Your mobile will attack people randomly.
ANTIGOOD      Your mobile will attack those of light alignment.
ANTIEVIL      Your mobile will attack those of dark alignment.
NPC      Your mobile will respond to interactive speech.
BARD      Your mobile can help you write a description or plan.
GUARD      Your mobile will guard the zone and respond to any unlawful acts.
SKILLMASTER      Your mobile can give people skill information.
SPELLMASTER      Your mobile can give people spell information.
MERCHANT      Your mobile can list, buy and sell items.

Skills & Actions.

Your mobile can randomly say and do things. If they are in combat, they can randomly execute skills and spells. Both actions and skills simply need an indicated frequency. This is again based on the scale from 0-60000 (since mobiles consider movement and action every 1 second). Therefore once again, 0 would be to never perform the action, and 60000 would be to perform the action every second. At 1000, the action will be performed about once per minute, and at 100 it will be performed about once every ten minutes. And so forth.

Quests & Unique Interaction.

In general, speech interaction with mobiles is based on flags. But sometimes you can carry on a unique conversation with an individual mobile. Some special mobiles will send you on quests, or be a part of an interactive quest. The process of creating such a mobile is very abstract, and should be written out in plain English, describing the entire quest idea in detail.

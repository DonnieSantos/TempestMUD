Character Stats And Balancing Items.

To make balanced equipment, you need to keep have a grasp of which characters have what kinds of stats. The following charts will give you an average of what those numbers might be for each basic type of class. You will have to make your own adjustments based on which sub-path you are. For instance, a Knight would have less attack power and health than a Warrior, but more defense and mana.

Also keep in mind the idea of "kits", which are equipment sets formed for a specific purpose. A Shadowblade for example might want to form a Damage Kit, in which they assemble equipment that adds the most damroll. Keep this in mind, so that you don't provide specialized classes with too much power in their area of interest, but give them at least a moderate amount of what they might be looking for.

These numbers represent Natural Stats, meaning with no equipment modifiers. Equipment in a Specific Full Kit should not exceed roughly 150% of the natural stat.

For example, a Level 99 Warrior in a hitpoint kit should not be able to get much more than (3600 * 1.5) which is 5400. If equipment exists that allows such a character to attain well over this amount (in this case, well over 5400 hp) then the equipment either needs some negative modifiers, or a reduction in the specified health bonus.

General Class Differences.

Warrior       --  All-round combat class. Well-protected and great damage.
Knight        --  More defense than a Warrior, less damage.
Paladin       --  More defense than a Warrior, less damage. Great resistance.
Berzerker     --  More damage than a Warrior, less defense.
Crusader      --  Best defense, worst damage.
Death Knight  --  Second-best damage. Average defense.
Anti Paladin  --  Best damage, worst defense.

Thief         --  Skill class. Less protection than a Warrior. Good damage.
Assassin      --  More damage than a Thief. No combat losses. Great damage.
Shadowblade   --  Better damage than an Assassin, less protection.
Ranger        --  Best bow-user. Good damage, protection, health and mana.
Rogue         --  Best protection. Slightly better than a Thief in all combat areas.
Mercenary     --  Balanced. Good damage, protection, health and mana.
Merchant      --  Non-combat oriented. Weak stats. Cannot equip most items.

Cleric        --  Healing class. Lots of wisdom and mana. Weak physically.
Druid         --  More health and defense than a Cleric. Same mana and spell power.
Monk          --  Best health and physical damage. Lowest mana and spell power.
Priest        --  Best mana and wisdom. Good spell power. Less health and defense.
Dark Cleric   --  More spell power, less mana. Average health and defense.
Healer        --  Non-combat oriented. Weak stats.
Alchemist     --  Non-combat oriented. Weak stats.

Mage          --  Magic class. Great spell power and resistance. Weakest physically.
Wizard        --  Second-best spell power. Good mana. Weak defense.
Illusionist   --  Less mana than a Cleric, more health and resistance.
Sorcerer      --  Balanced. Has good health, mana, defense, and spell power.
Warlock       --  Best spell power. Lower mana and defense.
Summoner      --  Non-combat oriented. Weak stats.
Shapeshifter  --  Non-combat oriented. Weak stats.
